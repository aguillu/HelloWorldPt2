Alyssa Guillu

Undergraduate Researcher at UChicago, Aspiring PhD Student in Psychology

Alyssa is a fourth year undergraduate student double majoring in psychology and linguistics at the University of Chicago. Currently, she conducts research that examines the form, function, and sequence organization of gesture in speech. She is particularly interested in investigating the role gesture plays in the spontaneous speech of children with brain injuries, and examining the broader relationship between language and cognition.
